=== CHUNK 28: Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics ===

Temperature is a measure of average kinetic energy: (3/2)k_B T per particle for a monatomic ideal gas. Absolute zero (0 K) is the theoretical temperature at which all thermal motion ceases. The Third Law of Thermodynamics states that entropy approaches a constant minimum as temperature approaches absolute zero. Heat capacity is the energy required to raise temperature by one degree: C = Q/ΔT. For constant volume: C_V = (3/2)nR for a monatomic ideal gas. For constant pressure: C_P = C_V + nR. The ratio of specific heats is γ = C_P/C_V. For monatomic gases, γ = 5/3. For diatomic gases, γ = 7/5. Adiabatic processes follow PV^γ = constant. Isothermal processes maintain constant temperature. Isobaric processes maintain constant pressure. Isovolumetric (isochoric) processes maintain constant volume. The efficiency of a Carnot engine is the maximum possible between two temperatures. No heat engine can exceed the Carnot efficiency. The coefficient of performance for a refrigerator is COP = Q_C/W. For a Carnot refrigerator, COP = T_C/(T_H − T_C). Heat pumps move heat from cold to hot reservoirs using work input. The entropy change for a reversible process is ΔS = ∫ dQ/T. For a free expansion of an ideal gas, ΔS = nR ln(V_f/V_i). Entropy always increases for irreversible processes in isolated systems. The number of microstates Ω for a given macrostate determines entropy: S = k_B ln Ω. The Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution describes molecular speeds in an ideal gas. The most probable speed is v_mp = √(2k_B T/m). The average speed is v_avg = √(8k_B T/πm). The root-mean-square speed is v_rms = √(3k_B T/m). Mean free path is the average distance between molecular collisions: λ = 1/(√2 π d² n_V). Diffusion rate depends on the concentration gradient and diffusion coefficient.

=== CHUNK 29: Mechanical Waves ===

The speed of a transverse wave on a stretched string is v = √(F_T/μ). The wave function for a traveling wave is y(x,t) = A sin(kx − ωt + φ). The wave number is k = 2π/λ. Angular frequency is ω = 2πf = 2π/T. Wave speed in terms of frequency and wavelength: v = fλ. The intensity of a spherical wave decreases as 1/r² from the source. Power transmitted by a wave on a string is P = ½μω²A²v. The superposition principle: when waves overlap, displacements add algebraically. Constructive interference occurs when phase difference is 0, 2π, 4π, ... Destructive interference occurs when phase difference is π, 3π, 5π, ... Beats occur when two waves of slightly different frequencies interfere. Beat frequency equals the absolute difference: f_beat = |f₁ − f₂|. A wave pulse inverts upon reflection from a fixed boundary. A wave pulse does not invert upon reflection from a free boundary. The fundamental frequency of a string fixed at both ends is f₁ = v/(2L). Harmonics are integer multiples of the fundamental: f_n = nf₁. For a pipe open at both ends: f_n = nv/(2L), n = 1,2,3,... For a pipe closed at one end: f_n = nv/(4L), n = 1,3,5,... (odd harmonics only). Resonance occurs when driving frequency matches a natural frequency. The quality factor Q measures sharpness of resonance: Q = ω₀/Δω. Fourier's theorem: any periodic wave can be expressed as a sum of sine and cosine functions. The Fourier transform decomposes non-periodic signals into frequency components. Sound intensity is I = P/A where P is power and A is area. The human ear can detect intensities from 10⁻¹² to 1 W/m². Ultrasound has frequencies above 20 kHz. Infrasound has frequencies below 20 Hz.

=== CHUNK 30: Electromagnetic Theory ===

Coulomb's constant k = 8.987551787 × 10⁹ N·m²/C². The permittivity of free space is ε₀ = 8.854 × 10⁻¹² C²/(N·m²). Electric field lines begin on positive charges and end on negative charges. The electric field inside a conductor in electrostatic equilibrium is zero. Excess charge on a conductor resides entirely on its surface. The electric field just outside a conductor is perpendicular to the surface. A Faraday cage shields its interior from external electric fields. The electric dipole moment magnitude is p = qd. The torque on a dipole in an electric field is τ = pE sin θ. The energy of a dipole in an electric field is U = −pE cos θ. The capacitance of an isolated conducting sphere of radius R is C = 4πε₀R. Dielectric constant κ is always greater than 1. The electric susceptibility is χ_e = κ − 1. The polarization vector P relates to the electric field: P = ε₀χ_e E. The electric displacement field is D = ε₀E + P = κε₀E. Energy density in a dielectric is u = ½κε₀E². The current density J relates to current: I = ∫ J·dA. Drift velocity of charge carriers is v_d = I/(nqA). Resistivity depends on temperature: ρ = ρ₀[1 + α(T−T₀)]. Superconductors have zero resistivity below a critical temperature T_c. The critical temperature for YBCO is about 92 K. The Meissner effect: superconductors expel magnetic fields. The magnetic permeability of free space is μ₀ = 4π × 10⁻⁷ T·m/A. Magnetic dipole moment of a current loop is μ = IA. The torque on a magnetic dipole in a magnetic field is τ = μB sin θ. Ferromagnetic materials have magnetic domains that can be aligned. The Curie temperature is where ferromagnetism disappears. For iron, the Curie temperature is 1043 K. Electromagnetic waves are transverse waves. The Poynting vector magnitude is S = EB/μ₀ for plane waves. The momentum flux of an EM wave is S/c. Radiation pressure from sunlight at Earth's orbit is approximately 5 × 10⁻⁶ Pa.

=== CHUNK 31: Relativistic and Quantum Extensions ===

The Lorentz transformation for coordinates: x' = γ(x − vt), t' = γ(t − vx/c²). The spacetime interval Δs² = (cΔt)² − (Δx)² is invariant under Lorentz transformations. Timelike intervals have Δs² > 0 and can be causally connected. Spacelike intervals have Δs² < 0 and cannot be causally connected. The relativistic velocity addition formula: u' = (u − v)/(1 − uv/c²). The relativistic Doppler factor is √((1+β)/(1−β)) for approaching sources. Cherenkov radiation occurs when a charged particle moves faster than light in a medium. The photoelectric effect proved that light energy is quantized. The work function of sodium is 2.28 eV. The work function of platinum is 6.35 eV. The stopping potential V₀ is related to maximum kinetic energy: eV₀ = K_max. The de Broglie wavelength of a 100 eV electron is about 0.12 nm. The Davisson-Germer experiment confirmed electron diffraction in 1927. The wave function ψ must be single-valued, continuous, and finite. The probability current density is j = (ℏ/2mi)(ψ*∇ψ − ψ∇ψ*). The expectation value of an operator Â is ⟨A⟩ = ∫ ψ*Âψ dx. The Hamiltonian operator corresponds to total energy: Ĥ = −(ℏ²/2m)∇² + V. The time-dependent Schrödinger equation is iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ. Stationary states have definite energy and evolve as ψ(t) = ψ(0)e^(−iEt/ℏ). A particle in an infinite square well has energies E_n = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²). The ground state has n=1 and nonzero energy — zero-point energy. The harmonic oscillator has equally spaced energy levels: E_n = (n+½)ℏω. Tunneling probability through a rectangular barrier decreases exponentially with barrier width. The scanning tunneling microscope uses quantum tunneling to image surfaces at atomic resolution. Spin is an intrinsic angular momentum with no classical analog. The spin quantum number for electrons is s = ½. The Stern-Gerlach experiment demonstrated quantization of spin. Spin-orbit coupling produces fine structure in atomic spectra. The Zeeman effect splits spectral lines in a magnetic field. The total angular momentum quantum number is j = l ± s. The Pauli exclusion principle explains the periodic table's structure. Fermi-Dirac statistics apply to fermions (half-integer spin). Bose-Einstein statistics apply to bosons (integer spin). A Bose-Einstein condensate forms when bosons occupy the same quantum ground state at ultra-low temperatures.

Quantum entanglement means the state of one particle cannot be described independently of another. Bell's inequality shows that quantum mechanics is incompatible with local hidden variable theories. Aspect's experiments in 1982 confirmed violation of Bell's inequality. Quantum computing uses superposition and entanglement for computation. A qubit can exist in a superposition of |0⟩ and |1⟩ states. The no-cloning theorem states that an unknown quantum state cannot be perfectly copied.
