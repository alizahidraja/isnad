Radioactive Decay
In 1928, Gamow identified quantum tunneling as the mechanism responsible for the radioactive decay of atomic nuclei.
Heobservedthatsomeisotopesofthorium,uranium,andbismuthdisintegratebyemitting α-particles(whicharedoubly
ionized helium atoms or, simply speaking, helium nuclei). In the process of emitting an α-particle, the original nucleus
is transformed into a new nucleus that has two fewer neutrons and two fewer protons than the original nucleus. The α

energies are of the same order of magnitude. This is about where the similarities between various isotopes end.
