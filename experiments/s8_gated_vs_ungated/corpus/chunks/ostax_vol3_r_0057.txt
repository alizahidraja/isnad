Barrier penetration by quantum wave functions was first analyzed theoretically by Friedrich Hund in 1927, shortly after
Schrӧdinger published the equation that bears his name. A year later, George Gamow used the formalism of quantum
mechanics to explain the radioactive α-decay of atomic nuclei as a quantum-tunneling phenomenon. The invention of
the tunnel diode in 1957 made it clear that quantum tunneling is important to the semiconductor industry. In modern
nanotechnologies, individual atoms are manipulated using a knowledge of quantum tunneling.
